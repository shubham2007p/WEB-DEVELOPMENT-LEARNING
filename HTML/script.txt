**HTML TEACHING STRUCTURE – 100% HTML (NO CSS/JS)**

***

### 1) Foundations and Document Anatomy
- What HTML is: purpose, markup, elements, attributes.
- Document skeleton: doctype, html, head, body.
- Minimal valid page: required pieces and why they matter.
- Global attributes: id, class, title, lang, dir, hidden, tabindex, data-*.
- Comments and whitespace: when and how to use.

Side topics later:
- Content categories: flow, phrasing, sectioning, interactive, embedded.
- Palpable content and empty elements.

***

### 2) Head and Metadata
- head basics: title, meta charset, viewport, description.
- Linking resources: link rel (icons, manifest), base URL.
- Favicons/app icons.

Side topics later:
- SEO-friendly metadata and social previews (og:, twitter:).
- Localization (lang, dir).

***

### 3) Text and Semantics
- Headings h1–h6: hierarchy and document outline.
- Paragraphs p and hr.
- Emphasis and meaning: em, strong, small, mark, cite, q, blockquote.
- Code elements: code, kbd, samp, var.
- time, abbr, data, dfn.

Side topics later:
- hgroup usage.
- Quotations best practices.

***

### 4) Links and Navigation
- a: href, target, download, rel.
- nav element: when to use.
- Internal anchors with id; skip links.

Side topics:
- Link semantics.
- base element effect.

***

### 5) Lists and Grouping
- ul, ol, li usage.
- Description lists dl, dt, dd.
- div for generic grouping.

Side topics:
- Nested lists.
- menu vs ul.

***

### 6) Sectioning and Page Structure
- section, article, header, footer, main, aside.
- Headings in each section.
- address for contact info.

Side topics:
- section vs article.
- headers/footers per section.

***

### 7) Images and Media
- img: src, alt, width/height, figure/figcaption.
- audio and video basics.
- Decorative vs informative.

Side topics:
- Accessibility of media.
- picture element concepts.

***

### 8) Tables
- table structure: caption, thead, tbody, tfoot, tr, th, td.
- scope on th.

Side topics:
- Multi-level headers.
- caption placement.

***

### 9) Forms
- form: action, method, name/value.
- label association: for/id, fieldset, legend.
- Inputs: text, email, url, number, checkbox, radio, file, hidden.
- textarea, select/option/optgroup, button.
- Validation attributes: required, minlength, maxlength, pattern.

Side topics:
- Grouping radios.
- Accessibility tips.

***

### 10) Interactive/Disclosure HTML Elements
- details/summary.
- dialog basics.
- meter and progress.

Side topics:
- Keyboard focus expectations.
- HTML-only behaviors.

***

### 11) Advanced Semantics & Content Models
- Content categories: flow vs phrasing.
- Inline vs block behavior and content models.
- Interactive content rules.

Side topics:
- How browsers fix invalid HTML.
- Palpable and empty elements.

***

### 12) Accessibility First (No ARIA/JS)
- Semantics for a navigable outline.
- Alt text rules.
- Focus order by source.

Side topics:
- Landmarks (header, nav, main, footer).
- When ARIA might be needed.

***

### 13) Templates & Headless HTML
- template element.
- slot/shadow DOM terms.

Side topics:
- Intro to Web Components.

***

### 14) Quality & Validation
- W3C validator usage.
- Common mistakes: invalid nesting, missing alt/labels.

Side topics:
- HTML vs DOM.
- Progressive enhancement.
